XR (Extended Reality) is an umbrella term covering all immersive technologies that blend the digital and physical worlds. Think of it as the family name for VR, AR, and MR.

üìñ Essential XR Terms (ELI5)

Display

FOV (Field of View)

Technical: The extent of the observable world seen at any given moment

ELI5: How much you can see without turning your head. Wider = more immersive. Human eyes = ~210¬∞. Most headsets = 90-110¬∞.

Display

Refresh Rate (Hz)

Technical: Number of times display updates per second

ELI5: How smooth motion looks. 90Hz = 90 pictures per second. Higher = smoother, less motion sickness. Quest 3 = 120Hz.

Display

IPD (Interpupillary Distance)

Technical: Distance between the centers of your pupils

ELI5: How far apart your eyes are. Headsets let you adjust lens spacing to match your IPD for clearer, more comfortable viewing.

Tracking

6DOF (Six Degrees of Freedom)

Technical: Tracking movement in 6 directions: forward/back, left/right, up/down, pitch, yaw, roll

ELI5: You can walk around, duck, lean, and turn naturally. 3DOF = only rotation (no walking).

Tracking

Inside-Out Tracking

Technical: Cameras on headset track position without external sensors

ELI5: Headset tracks itself using built-in cameras. No base stations needed! Quest 3 and Vision Pro use this.

Tracking

Outside-In Tracking

Technical: External sensors (base stations) track headset position

ELI5: Cameras in room corners watch you move. More accurate but requires setup. Valve Index uses this.

Features

Passthrough

Technical: Real-time video feed from external cameras displayed inside headset

ELI5: See the real world through your VR headset's cameras. Useful for safety and mixed reality. Quest 3 = full color!

Features

Hand Tracking

Technical: Computer vision system that detects and maps hand/finger positions

ELI5: Use your bare hands instead of controllers. Headset sees and tracks your fingers. Vision Pro relies heavily on this.

Features

Eye Tracking

Technical: Sensors detect where you're looking for interaction and rendering optimization

ELI5: Headset knows where you're looking. Look at button = click. Also makes graphics better where you're focused.

Movement

Locomotion

Technical: Methods for moving through virtual environments

ELI5: How you move in VR. Options: walk naturally, teleport (point & jump), smooth movement (joystick), or room-scale (physical walking).

Movement

Room-Scale VR

Technical: VR experience that tracks user movement within a defined physical space

ELI5: Walk around your room and it matches in VR. Need clear space. Guardian boundaries keep you safe from walls.

Hardware

Standalone vs Tethered

Technical: Self-contained system vs PC/console-connected headset

ELI5: Standalone = all-in-one, wireless (Quest 3). Tethered = plugs into PC/PS5 for more power (Index, PSVR2).

Hardware

Foveated Rendering

Technical: Rendering technique that displays full detail only where user is looking

ELI5: Like your eyes - sharp in center, blurry at edges. Saves GPU power. Requires eye tracking. Vision Pro uses this.

Development

SDK (Software Development Kit)

Technical: Collection of software tools for creating applications for specific platform

ELI5: Toolbox for developers to build VR apps. Meta has Quest SDK, Apple has Vision Pro SDK, etc.

Development

OpenXR

Technical: Open standard for VR/AR application development across platforms

ELI5: Universal language for VR apps. Write once, works on Quest, Index, PSVR2, etc. Like USB for VR.

Audio

Spatial Audio

Technical: 3D audio that simulates sound sources with directional and positional accuracy

ELI5: Sounds come from specific directions/distances. Hear footsteps behind you, voices to your left. Dramatically increases immersion.

Content

Metaverse

Technical: Persistent, shared virtual worlds where users interact socially and economically

ELI5: Virtual universe you can visit anytime. Like Second Life but in VR. Examples: VRChat, Rec Room, Horizon Worlds.

Content

Spatial Computing

Technical: Computing paradigm where applications are aware of and interact with 3D space

ELI5: Apps that exist in 3D space around you. Apple's term for Vision Pro. Windows float in your room instead of on flat screen.
